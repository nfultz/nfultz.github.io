Another awesome paper is System R: Relational Approach to Database Management. Many things have changed since then, but not enough to make any of what it says less relevant. You can't get rid of transaction/recovery management, query optimizer, etc. https://www.seas.upenn.edu/~zives/cis650/papers/System-R.PDF (edited) 

Even though indexing and storage is important, I still think ARIES, describing interactions of WAL and transaction processing engine is one of the most important ones: https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~brewer/cs262/Aries.pdf it really helps you to understand recovery mechanism and notion of time. I think @Nikolay will like it a lot, judging form the questions about zookeeper. It unveils what really happens when two or more transactions actually collide and do override each other's effects, or what happens during recovery: which records are persisted and which are discarded.
